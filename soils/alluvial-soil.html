Soil Type

Alluvial Soil

Alluvial soils are formed by river deposition and are often fertile. Texture can vary from sandy to clayey depending on deposition conditions.

Key Properties

  • Often good fertility and nutrient reserves
  • Texture: variable

Suitable Crops (Examples)

  • Paddy, sugarcane, banana, vegetables

Management Tips

  • Maintain nutrients with balanced fertilization
  • Manage salinity in low-lying/irrigated areas if present
